1 scope 
this recommendation specifies a framework of trusted inter-cloud computing and relevant use cases, based 
on the framework of inter-cloud computing [itu-t y.3511]. the scope of this recommendation includes: 
– an overview of trusted inter-cloud computing;  
– general requirements for trusted inter-cloud; 
– requirements for governance of trusted inter-cloud; 
– requirements for management of trusted inter-cloud; 
– requirements for resiliency of trusted inter-cloud; 
– requirements for security and confidentiality of trusted inter-cloud.

3 definitions 
3.1 terms defined elsewhere 
this recommendation uses the following terms defined elsewhere: 
3.1.1 availability [itu-t y.3500]: property of being accessible and usable upon demand by an authorized 
entity. 
3.1.2 confidentiality [itu-t y.3500]: property that information is not made available or disclosed to 
unauthorized individuals, entities, or processes. 
3.1.3 cloud computing [itu-t y.3500]: paradigm for enabling network access to a scalable and elastic pool 
of shareable physical or virtual resources with self-service provisioning and administration on-demand. 
note – examples of resources include servers, operating systems, networks, software, applications, and storage 
equipment.
